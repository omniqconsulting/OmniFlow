Who Can See What

Plain-language breakdown of what each role can access and do
Admin
Can See
  • All tickets across all employees and managers
  • All checklists and compliance data
  • All Flow Board flows, stages, and tickets
  • Full employee list and performance KPIs
  • Inventory dashboard and reconciliation alerts
  • All linked entities across the system
Can Do
  • Create tickets and assign to anyone
  • Close and delete tickets
  • Configure flows, stages, and sub-modules
  • Add and manage employees, roles, departments
  • Change plan settings (via Super Admin)
  • Access all Setup options
Manager
Can See
  • All tickets assigned to their direct team only
  • Team checklists and compliance for their team
  • Flow Board stages where their team members are assignees
  • Team KPI dashboard
  • Linked entities on tickets they can see
Can Do
  • Create tickets and assign to their team
  • Reassign tickets within their team
  • Flag tickets for Admin attention
  • Override Flow Board stage transitions (within 2-hour window)
  • Log comments and add helpers on team tickets
Employee
Can See
  • Only their own assigned tickets
  • Only their own checklist assignments
  • Flow Board stages where they are the current assignee
  • Their own KPI dashboard
  • Linked entities on their own tickets (read-only)
Can Do
  • Mark their own tickets as Done
  • Log delays with a reason (does not change status)
  • Add comments on their own tickets
  • Request help (adds a helper to their ticket)
  • Flag a ticket for management attention
Helper exception: An Employee who is added as a Helper on another employee's ticket can see and interact with that ticket — this is the only exception to the "own tickets only" rule.
